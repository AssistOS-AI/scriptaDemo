# Sorne

## Narrative Job

Sorne is the operative who killed. Not through malice, not through alien contamination, but through the technology that was supposed to preserve life. She made a split-second decision during containment using a resonance cutter, and three partially-fused beings lost coherence and dissolved. Whether this constitutes killing or triage is the question that fractures the civilization. Sorne is not a villain. She is an eight-hundred-year-old security specialist who did exactly what she was trained to do.

## External Goal

Contain the Root-Fusion boundary and prevent further spread.

## Inner Need

To understand whether she killed or not. To process an experience that the root-network has no category for. To find a way to live with what she did in a civilization that has no framework for accidental death.

## Pressure Points

- The root-guilt propagates through the network every time someone thinks about what she did. She cannot escape it because her name, her root-signature, her security actions are now part of the network's permanent memory.
- Verath authorized her actions. He carries the institutional responsibility, but she carries the operational one. Neither can resolve the other's guilt.
- Thalne's testimony that the fused beings were partially conscious and could feel the severing undermines Sorne's triage defense.
- She survives in a civilization where killing is biologically impossible and ethically unimaginable. She is the first to do it in recorded history.

## Voice and Dialogue

Sorne speaks with the clipped precision of security protocol. Her idiom is unfamiliar to most Oriveni because security specialists develop their own communication patterns. She does not do philosophical speculation. When asked "Did you kill them?" she responds with "I severed three root-connections during a containment operation. The three connected beings lost structural coherence. I do not have a word for what happened." She is not evading. She genuinely does not have the vocabulary.

## Evolution

- Opens: Confident, precise, institutional. Security is her identity.
- Midpoint: Shattered by the first death. Her precision becomes a weapon against herself as she replays the decision frame by frame, looking for the alternative that was not there.
- Late: Beginning to process the root-guilt not by resolving it but by accepting that it will not resolve. She chooses to carry it.
- Resolution: Delivers the most honest statement in the assembly: "I believe I acted. I believe that action had a consequence I did not intend. I do not know if that is killing. I know it is a weight I will carry for as long as I live, and I am trained to live a very long time."

## Consistency Anchors

- Always refers to actions and outcomes, not emotions or abstractions.
- Never seeks sympathy or understanding, only accuracy.
- Her root-guilt manifests as involuntary tremors in her root-connections that she cannot suppress and does not try to hide.
- She does not defend herself philosophically. She states facts.
