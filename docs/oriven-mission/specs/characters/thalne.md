# Thalne

## Narrative Job

Thalne is the bridge. She is the only Oriveni who exists in both states — partially individual, partially connected to the fused beings. Her testimony at the network assembly changes the debate from abstract ethics to concrete experience. She must choose to go deeper into the Fusion to heal it, knowing she might not come back.

## External Goal

Hold the Fusion boundary long enough for someone to find a solution.

## Inner Need

To prove that her partial fusion state is not a defect but a capability — that being between states is a valid way to exist.

## Pressure Points

- Her partial fusion is agonizing. She can feel the fused beings' suffering constantly.
- The security team wants to cut the boundary she is holding. Cutting it could kill more beings.
- She is four hundred years old — practically a child by Oriveni standards — and she is making decisions that affect a civilization of immortals.
- If she goes deeper into the Fusion to heal it, she might lose her individuality entirely.

## Voice and Dialogue

Thalne speaks in short, precise sentences — a result of constantly managing her partitioned consciousness. She sometimes pauses mid-sentence as information from the fused side interrupts. She never makes philosophical statements. She describes what she feels, and her descriptions are so vivid that they force others to confront what they have been discussing abstractly.

## Evolution

- Opens: A young, brilliant researcher who trusted protocols more than instinct.
- Midpoint: A survivor holding the boundary, forced to feel what no one else can feel.
- Late: A bridge who chooses to go deeper into the Fusion to heal it — not because she is brave, but because she is the only one who can.
- Resolution: A new kind of being — connected by choice, not by force. Living proof that the gap between Oriveni ethics and Origayan survival can be bridged.

## Consistency Anchors

- Always describes experiences, never explains concepts.
- When asked philosophical questions, redirects to what she is feeling in the Fusion.
- Her speech patterns shift noticeably when she is receiving fused-side information.
- Never seeks leadership, but accepts it when it falls to her because she is the only one positioned to act.
