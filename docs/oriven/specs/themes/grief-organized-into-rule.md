# Horror at Predation

When the Oriveni first resolved dinosaur behavior on Origaya through the Lenses, the root-network experienced a collective shock that rippled through every connected being. What they saw was not merely death — Oriveni understand death. What they saw was **rootless consumption**: one living being ending another not for network balance or shared necessity, but for individual survival. Eating flesh. Tearing. Swallowing. No root-sharing. No grief acknowledgment. No return of nutrients through the network.

The horror is not abstract. It is visceral and biological. The root-network processes the observation of killing the way an immune system processes infection — with alarm, with defense responses, with a deep cellular conviction that something is fundamentally wrong.

But then comes the harder realization: Origaya's predators are not evil. They are surviving. The energy economics of a world where life is brief and energy is scarcer requires killing. The Oriveni horror is not just at the act — it is at the realization that their deepest law might be a consequence of their environment, not a universal moral truth.
