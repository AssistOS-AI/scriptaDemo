# Long Thinking vs. Short Violence

Oriveni think across millennia. Their debates last decades. Their buildings grow over centuries. An individual decision can take seasons of shared deliberation through the root-network.

Origaya operates on completely different timescales. Dinosaurs live, hunt, kill, and die within individual lifetimes measured in mere decades. Their survival strategies are fast, brutal, and immediate.

This theme explores several tensions:

- **Can long-lived beings truly understand short-lived beings?** Oriveni compassion operates on geological time. Origayan predation operates on biological time. The gap is not just quantitative but qualitative.
- **Is patience a luxury of abundance?** Oriveni can afford not to kill because their environment provides enough energy through concentrated solar input and geothermal warmth. On Origaya, relative scarcity makes violence necessary.
- **What does it mean to be ethical in a universe where most life must kill?** The Oriveni discover that their ethics may be a product of privilege, not principle — and this realization is the deepest horror of all.
