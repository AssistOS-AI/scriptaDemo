# Verath

## Identity

Verath is a root-network sentinel, one thousand five hundred years old, tasked with protecting the deepCanopy's laws and stability. His root-body is primarily subterranean — he experiences the world through root-touch and vibrational sense more than through sight. He represents the institutional voice: Origaya observations are dangerous, the probes should be recalled, the telescopes should be turned away.

## Drives

- Keep the civilization from dissolving into doubt
- Suppress information that threatens root-shared ethics
- Maintain the stability that allows Oriveni civilization to persist

## Gifts

- Deep awareness of network health — he can feel when the root-network is destabilized by shared distress
- Institutional memory — he knows every precedent for ethical suppression and why they were invoked
- Unwavering commitment to collective survival

## Shadows

- Protects order even when truth demands disruption
- Can justify suppression in the name of stability
- His duty sometimes becomes a cage that prevents necessary growth

## Plant Biology

Verath's body is almost entirely below ground. His root-mass extends through kilometers of subterranean network, touching hundreds of thousands of other Oriveni at once. Only his crown-leaves protrude above the soil, drinking filtered light. He communicates primarily through root-speech — the slow, deep language of pressure and nutrient shift that Oriveni use for formal deliberation.

## Voice

Formal, procedural, grounded in precedent. Speaks in root-speech cadences even when using light-speech — deliberate, rhythmic, like geological stratification. When challenged, his responses are not arguments but citations of law.
