# Aethon

## Identity

Aethon is a four-thousand-year-old walking tree-sage, the oldest living keeper of the Root Archive — the embodied memory of Oriveni ethical precedent. His trunk-body stands twelve meters tall in the deepCanopy's central cavern, root-tendrils reaching into the network that connects all Oriveni life. He has watched civilizations of root and bark rise and settle. His bark is deeply fissured, his canopy vast, his root-network connections numbering in the millions.

## Drives

- Preserve and protect Oriveni ethical inheritance
- Understand whether the law against killing is universal or merely local
- Resolve the contradiction between root-shared ethics and the evidence from Origaya

## Gifts

- Persistence across ages — he has outlived every crisis Oriven has faced
- Root-archive memory — he carries precedent like a living library
- Patience shaped by millennia of slow thinking

## Shadows

- Confuses preservation with wisdom
- Tends to value continuity over truth when continuity is threatened
- His age makes him slow to adapt, but when he does adapt, the shift is seismic

## Plant Biology

Aethon moves slowly and deliberately. His root-feet can grip stone, his trunk-body can rotate toward light or sound, and his canopy-hands (modified branch-tips) can manipulate tools with precision. He thinks in seasonal cycles — a single decision may take months of root-network deliberation. His consciousness extends through his root-tendrils, giving him access to the shared memory and emotional state of every connected Oriveni.

## Voice

Slow, measured, rich with precedent and analogy. Speaks in long sentences that unfold like root growth. When moved, his prose becomes unexpectedly direct — the ancient tree delivering a verdict.
