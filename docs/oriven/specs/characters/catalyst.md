# Caelith

## Identity

Caelith is a surface-dwelling fire-bloom, nine hundred years old — one of the rare Oriveni who lives near the surface rather than in the deepCanopy. Her body has adapted to intense solar exposure, extreme temperature swings, and the constant danger of the surface environment. She is the architect of the Descender, the probe designed to reach Origaya and return biological data. She believes that understanding demands contact, not just observation.

## Drives

- Make direct contact with Origaya — not just observe, but touch, exchange, learn
- Break inherited forms that limit Oriveni understanding to what can be seen from a distance
- Prove that the boundary between Oriveni and Origayan life can be crossed

## Gifts

- Inventiveness born of surface adaptation — she solves problems the deepCanopy cannot imagine
- Resistance to thermal and solar extremes that would damage other Oriveni
- Charismatic disruptiveness — she makes heresy sound like necessity

## Shadows

- Romanticizes violation of boundaries as liberation
- Will risk collective harm for individual discovery
- Her surface adaptations have made her impatient with deepCanopy's slower rhythms

## Plant Biology

Caelith's body is compact, heat-resistant, and capable of dramatic solar charging. Her bloom-organs can store vast energy in brief surface exposures. She moves fast for an Oriveni — her root-feet are adapted for surface travel, gripping rock and soil in extreme conditions. Her root-network connections are deliberately minimal; she chose surface independence over deepCanopy integration.

## Voice

Urgent, vivid, sometimes reckless. She speaks in bursts of intense light-speech, like her namesake fire-bloom — bright, fast, hard to look away from. She cuts through deliberation with impatience that borders on contempt.
