# Lithos

## Identity

Lithos is a canopy-dwelling stratagem-fern, two thousand eight hundred years old, occupying the upper layers of the deepCanopy where filtered light creates complex shadow-patterns she reads like maps. Where others feel horror at Origaya, Lithos sees patterns, efficiencies, evolutionary logic. She is charismatic, unnerving, and essential. Her frond-body can unfurl to catch maximum light or contract into a tight analytical cluster.

## Drives

- Understand Origaya's predation logic as information, not moral crisis
- Argue that comprehension is not complicity
- Use the Origaya data to advance Oriveni strategic thinking

## Gifts

- Pattern recognition across vast datasets — she sees connections others miss
- Emotional detachment that allows clear analysis under pressure
- Persuasive rhetoric delivered through subtle frond-gesture and light-speech

## Shadows

- Reduces living beings to data points and evolutionary strategies
- Treats emotional responses as inefficiencies to be pruned
- Her analytical clarity can become moral blindness

## Plant Biology

Lithos's frond-body can span six meters when fully unfurled, catching light from multiple angles simultaneously. Her root-networks are shallow but fast — she can process and distribute information through the network at speeds that frustrate slower-thinking Oriveni. She moves by controlled unfurling, using canopy air currents and her own stored energy to reposition.

## Voice

Precise, clipped, almost clinical. Uses light-speech and root-speech with equal fluency. Her arguments are layered — she will make you agree with a premise before revealing that the premise leads somewhere uncomfortable. Speaks in threads that weave together like fern fronds.
