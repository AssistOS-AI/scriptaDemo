# Mirel

## Identity

Mirel is a young shoot-intellect, barely three centuries old — still supple, still forming, still capable of radical growth-pattern shifts. She operates the Origaya Lenses, the telescope array that first resolved dinosaur predation on Earth. She is the one who first identified hunting behavior in dinosaurs and named it "rootless consumption" — killing without nutrient-sharing, without network acknowledgment, without grief. She cannot stop watching Origaya.

## Drives

- Find a pattern that makes Origaya's predation legible without destroying Oriveni moral frameworks
- Keep watching, even when watching hurts
- Understand whether compassion is possible without killing being necessary

## Gifts

- Exceptional light-speech and optical sensitivity — her lens-body is adapted for distance observation
- Youthful adaptability — she can still change her growth patterns in response to new information
- Empathic resonance through the root-network — she feels what she observes

## Shadows

- Treats obsession as moral necessity
- Risks root-network infection by processing grief without adequate support
- Her need to make suffering legible can become a form of paralysis

## Plant Biology

Mirel's body is built for light: slender, flexible, with specialized lens-organs at her crown that can resolve distant objects with extraordinary precision. She stores solar energy efficiently, needing less surface time than most Oriveni. Her root-networks are growing but shallow — she has not yet developed the deep, patient connections of elders like Aethon.

## Voice

Quick, questioning, often elliptical. She speaks in fragments that dart like light through canopy gaps. When moved by what she sees through the lenses, her speech becomes longer, more rooted, as if Origaya is sinking taproots into her consciousness.
