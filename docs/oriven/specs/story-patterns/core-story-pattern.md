# Core Story Pattern: Investigation Through Ruins

## Pattern Description

The Oriveni investigate Origaya as though examining a ruin — but the "ruin" is a thriving world built on principles antithetical to their own. Each observation uncovers not just data but a moral challenge. Every clue about Origayan predation also reveals something about Oriveni civilization that was invisible before.

## Application

- Origaya is the "ruin" being investigated — not because it is destroyed, but because its operating principles are alien to Oriveni understanding
- Each observation peels back a layer of assumption about what is natural, what is ethical, and what is merely convenient
- The investigation is not just scientific — it is moral. Understanding Origaya means understanding what Oriveni ethics is made of
- The "ruin" fights back: every attempt to categorize Origayan predation creates new categories that threaten Oriveni self-understanding

## Key Pressure Note

Every observation of Origaya also reveals who in Oriveni society wanted that truth buried.
