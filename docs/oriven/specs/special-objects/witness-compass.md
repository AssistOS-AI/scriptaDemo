# The Root Archive

Aethon's four-thousand-year-old tree-body is not just a living organism — it is the living memory of Oriveni civilization. Every precedent, every ethical decision, every crisis and resolution is encoded in its tissue. When Aethon searches for guidance, he does not consult a text — he grows toward the relevant precedent, feeling it rise through his root-structure like a memory returning to consciousness.

The Root Archive is also Aethon's vulnerability. If he is damaged, the archive is damaged. If he is wrong, the archive enshrines his error. When the Origaya crisis reveals that the archive has no precedent for this situation, the silence is felt across the entire root-network — not just as absence, but as a four-thousand-year-old being having nothing to say.
