# Ecological Reciprocity

The guiding wisdom of Oriveni civilization:

Every being is connected to every other through the root-network. What one takes, one returns. What one suffers, all feel. This is not metaphor — it is the biological substrate of Oriveni life.

The principle of ecological reciprocity means:
- No being ends another. Energy is shared through the network, not taken by force.
- Grief is processed collectively. No being mourns alone.
- Knowledge is distributed, not hoarded. Every insight propagates through root-connections.
- Growth is cooperative. Competitive growth is mediated by network feedback.

This wisdom is the foundation of Oriveni ethics. When they discover Origaya, they discover a world where none of these principles hold — and the question arises: is ecological reciprocity a universal moral truth, or merely what happens when life evolves in conditions of abundance?
