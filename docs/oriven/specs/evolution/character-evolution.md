# Character Evolution

## Aethon's Arc: From Inherited Certainty to Chosen Commitment

Aethon begins as the keeper of an unbroken ethical tradition. When Origaya challenges that tradition, his evolution is not from certainty to doubt — it is from inherited certainty to chosen commitment. He ends by choosing the same ethics he started with, but now he chooses them with full knowledge that they are contingent. This makes his commitment stronger, not weaker.

## Lithos's Arc: From Detached Analysis to Accountable Understanding

Lithos begins as the strategist who processes horror as data. When her analysis becomes the justification for actions she did not intend, she must confront the fact that understanding has consequences. She does not become more emotional — she becomes more accountable.

## Verath's Arc: From Enforced Law to Chosen Law

Verath begins as the enforcer of a law that is both moral and biological. When the biological foundation of that law is questioned, he must decide whether to enforce a principle he no longer believes is universal. He chooses to enforce — not because it is natural, but because it is necessary.

## Mirel's Arc: From Horrified Observer to Understanding Witness

Mirel begins as the one who first saw predation and named it with horror. She ends as the one who continues to observe with comprehension — not approval, not acceptance, but a willingness to see what is rather than what she wishes were.

## Caelith's Arc: From Boundary-Breaker to Boundary-Aware

Caelith begins as the one who wants to break through limits. She ends understanding that boundaries exist for reasons — not all of which are arbitrary. Her transgression has changed what is possible, but she has also learned that possibility does not equal permission.
