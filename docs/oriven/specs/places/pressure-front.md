# The Origaya Observatory

The Observatory is where Mirel's Origaya Lenses are mounted — a formation on the Charging Plains where gaps in Venus's cloud layer allow telescopic observation of Earth. This is where the first horror was seen, where Mirel first identified dinosaur predation, and where the ethical crisis is most acute.

## Key Features

- **The Lenses** — crystalline optical arrays grown over centuries, precise enough to resolve surface features on a planet tens of millions of kilometers away
- **The Data Pools** — root-network junctions near the Lenses where observation data is processed and distributed through the deepCanopy
- **The Shield Formation** — protective rock overhangs that allow Oriveni to make observations during brief safe windows in the surface conditions
- **The memorial roots** — young growths placed by Oriveni who come to observe Origaya and are moved to leave living markers of their emotional response

## Atmosphere

The Observatory is simultaneously the most important and most dangerous place in Oriveni consciousness. It is where knowledge arrives that cannot be unlearned. Standing near the Lenses, an Oriveni can feel the entire root-network tremble with shared distress when new observations confirm predation.
