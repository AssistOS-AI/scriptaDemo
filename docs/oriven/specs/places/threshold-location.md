# The Charging Plains

The Charging Plains are Venus's surface — a landscape of extremes. Scorched by daytime temperatures that would vaporize animal life, frozen at night, bombarded by solar radiation. For Oriveni, the surface is a necessary danger: they must make brief forays upward to charge their energy reserves in direct sunlight.

## Key Features

- **Solar charging stations** — ancient formations where Oriveni have stood for millennia, absorbing concentrated solar energy
- **The Transition Zone** — the geological layers between deepCanopy and surface, where temperature and radiation shift dramatically
- **The Observatory Shelf** — a stable formation where Mirel's Origaya Lenses are mounted, pointed skyward through gaps in Venus's cloud layer
- **Surface scar-gardens** — hardy surface plants that have adapted to extremes, forming ecological bridges between the underground world and the hostile sky

## Atmosphere

Blinding light, oppressive heat, then punishing cold. The sky is a thick ochre haze. Standing on the Charging Plains feels like standing inside a furnace that occasionally becomes a freezer. For Oriveni, this is where they confront the raw energy source that makes their underground civilization possible — and where they begin to understand why Origayan life, with its brief, intense existences, might need different strategies for survival.
