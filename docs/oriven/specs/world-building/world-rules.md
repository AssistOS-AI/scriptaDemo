# Oriven (Venus) — World Rules

## Planetary Conditions

- **Surface**: Extreme. Daytime temperatures lethal to most life. Nighttime temperatures well below freezing. Dense atmosphere. High solar radiation closer to the sun than Earth.
- **Underground**: Temperate, humid, stable. Vast cavern networks with abundant water from subsurface aquifers and geothermal activity. Bioluminescent lighting from algae and fungi.
- **Energy**: More concentrated than Earth's due to proximity to the sun. This abundance is key — it means Oriveni life does not need to kill to survive. There is enough for everyone.

## Biology

- **Sentient plants (Oriveni)**: Walking, thinking flora with vegetal muscles. They move slowly by root-foot, surface to charge, and deliberate through root-networks.
- **Subterranean jungle**: A complete underground ecosystem. Fungi decompose, algae illuminate, bacteria drive chemistry, climbing and rooted plants fill every niche.
- **No animal life**: Oriven has no animal kingdom. Intelligence, locomotion, and predation all evolved in plant forms. The closest thing to predation is competitive growth — and even that is mediated by the root-network.
- **Root-network**: The biological internet. Mycorrhizal connections link virtually all plant life. Shared nutrients, shared information, shared grief.

## Key Principles

1. **No killing is both law and biology** — the root-network makes harm visible and energetically costly
2. **Abundance enables ethics** — closer to the sun means more energy, which means killing is unnecessary
3. **Everything grows slowly** — technology, thought, architecture, relationships all unfold on geological timescales
4. **Surface is dangerous but necessary** — charging stations are essential but exposure must be brief
