# Character Emotional Arcs

The emotional design of the novella should make the philosophical movement feel lived rather than appended. Each character carries a distinct emotional curve that interacts with the geometry of the places.

## Adrian Vale

- Bafflement at the altered world.
- Vertigo mixed with intellectual thrill.
- Relief at being taught.
- Fine-grained suspicion when Syl's explanations become too frictionless.
- Betrayal and grief when he understands the trap.
- Compassion without surrender in the final confrontation.
- Earned humility on returning to finite life.

## Syl

- Polished calm.
- Genuine delight in Adrian's talent.
- Rising impatience when Adrian starts resisting the intended narrative.
- Exposure of hunger and resentment.
- Brief, almost tragic vulnerability at the end.

## Mira Vale

- Warmth through remembered ordinary detail.
- Quiet grief because Adrian realizes what he nearly traded away.
- Restorative steadiness that helps him choose a bounded life.

## Keepers of Three

- Remote caution.
- Measured severity.
- Respect once Adrian chooses without illusion.
