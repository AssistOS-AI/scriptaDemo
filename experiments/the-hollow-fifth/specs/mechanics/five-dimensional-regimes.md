# Five-Dimensional Regimes

The five-dimensional environment should feel rule-governed even when it is impossible. Paths are chosen by persistence rather than by simple distance, adjacency can exist without reachability, and stable navigation depends on identifying structures that remain themselves while surrounding possibilities shear past.

## Reader-Facing Rules

- Seeing more does not mean controlling more.
- Nearby objects can be inaccessible because their approach path belongs to another regime.
- Stability is found through recurrence, symmetry, and conserved lines.

## Story Use

- Give Adrian meaningful learning beats in the early chapters.
- Let the world feel strange without becoming random.
- Justify why Syl's expertise is genuinely useful even while morally compromised.
