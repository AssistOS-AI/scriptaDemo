# Cael Veyr

Cael Veyr is an expeditionary continuant of the Quiet Array, a civilization of cartographers and treaty engineers that vanished after dispersing itself into archival geometry. He inhabits both a humanoid diplomatic body and the courier warship `Mnemonic Dart`, thinking in branching routes while retaining an unusual moral bias toward plural outcomes. By the standards of dead empires, he is adventurous rather than doctrinal.

## Narrative Function

- Protagonist and point of strategic orientation.
- Explorer who becomes a constitutional actor.
- Coalition builder in a story where centralization is always tempting.

## Strengths

- Exceptional route improvisation through damaged relay space.
- Strong tolerance for uncertainty without surrendering rigor.
- Ability to hear an enemy's logic clearly without becoming seduced by it.

## Weakness

Cael tends to assume that sufficiently intelligent minds can be persuaded by proof once the true structure of the problem is visible. The novel should keep demonstrating that intelligence does not erase appetite.

## Arc

He begins as a messenger carrying information about the reopening of the Wells. He ends as one of the architects of the order that will govern how civilizations return.
